Zone Descriptions

Zonation Schemes â€“ Before you begin collecting data define your surroundings in one of the available zonation schemes: river, estuary or terrestrial. The zones are approximately 100 m wide unless there are obvious breaks. Take the time to describe the zones well enough that someone else can use the same scheme from the same location. It is quite possible that all the zones in the scheme will not be visible so will not be part of your description. Save your description in a document outside of the application, for instance a WORD doc in Docs to Go.

River:

If you are on the shores of a river you will be in either zone 6 or zone 4. This zone will extend about 50 m on either side of you or to an obvious break in the landscape. Find something approximately 100 m beyond that break to define the zones to your right and left. It will be useful if the zones across the river have approximately the same breaks. The depth of the zone will be as far as you can see. The river itself will be zones 2+, 2, 5, 8 and 8+.

If you are in a boat, the zones are defined relative to your current location and will therefore be shifting continuously (unless you are anchored). You will not be able to pre-define the zones, so use approximate distances. In a serpentine river it will be difficult to see beyond the 3 central zones on each bank and in the river.

Estuary:

If you are on the shores of a river you will be in either zone 6 or zone 4. This zone will extend about 50 m on either side of you or to an obvious break in the landscape. Find something approximately 100 m beyond that break to define the zones to your right and left. It will be useful if the zones across the river have approximately the same breaks. The depth of the zone will be as far as you can see. The river itself will be zones 2+, 2, 5, 8 and 8+.

If you are in a boat, the zones are defined relative to your current location and will therefore be shifting continuously (unless you are anchored). You will not be able to pre-define the zones, so use approximate distances. In a serpentine river it will be difficult to see beyond the 3 central zones on each bank and in the river.

Terrestrial:

This schema is the most flexible. It is assumed that you are not moving during observations, but if you do, your zones will shift. You are always in zone 5. If you are mobile, try to use regular 100 m by 100 m zones. If you are stationary, you can define the zones in a way that makes sense to the landscape.
